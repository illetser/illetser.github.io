WELCOME TO MY WORLD!!!

Hi there! I'm James.

Thank you for visiting my site. I currently live in Chicago where I help companies find their most important asset, their people.

My main motivations are: (1) to be with my family and people I care about, (2) to do something fun, exciting, and impactful, and (3) to pursue a long-term career in entrepreneurship, where I can build companies that change the way the world works.

Originally from St. Louis, I attended St. Louis Priory High School and graduated in 2004. I played varsity hockey, golf, and football.

I then attended Villanova University in Philadelphia, PA where I studied International Business, Management, and Entrepreneurship. During my time at Nova, I also was a four-year starting golatender on their Division I ECHA ice hockey team.

Professional Summary

After graduating in 2008, I moved back to St. Louis where I started working at DHR International, a global executive search firm. I joined as an Associate in the Private Equity/Venture Capital Practice where I supported the Managing Director in executive C-level search projects for PE-backed portfolio companies.

In 2010, I was transferred to the global headquarters in Chicago and promoted to Senior Associate. I spent the next year in a similar capacity, yet had more autonomy in managing and executive the various search assignments.

In mid-2011, I was offered a tremendous opportunity to move to Hong Kong as an expatriate. It was a two-year assignment to serve as the Talent Acquisition Director for Asia/Pacific responsible for developing and deplying the firm's regional growth strategy across Asia. These 2+ years in Hong Kong were arguably the most rewarding years of my life to date, both professionally and personally. After my assignment in late 2013, I made the difficult decision to leave DHR International and pursue my entrpreneurial passion with an early-stage technology startup.

Before returning to the U.S. in late-2013, I took a two-month sabattical and traveled to Bali, Indonesia and Costa Rica. After coming back to reality, I joined Groove Technologies, an early-stage technology startup. For the next two years, I worked with the co-founders to develop a new enterprise recruiting platform and native mobile app where candidates could browse and rate new opportunities. We successfully raised a $1M seed round and moved the HQ to San Francisco, CA. At that time, I learned I was accepted to Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management and was therefore unable to relocate to SF. So, in July 2015, I joined CareerBuilder as an Enterprise Account Executive partnering with Fortune 500 organizations to improve talent acquisition processes and leverage software and data to improve recruiter efficiencies.

Outside of work and school, I enjoy spending my time on the ice at Johnny's Ice House downtown where I play in a competitive men's league. I also serve as the Co-President of the Villanova's Alumni Association Chicago Chapter.
